Chest Fly
Chest Fly is a strength exercise that primarily targets the chest muscles (pectoralis major) by mimicking a “hugging” motion. It involves extending the arms outward and then bringing them together in front of the body while keeping the elbows slightly bent. This movement helps stretch and contract the chest muscles, enhancing muscle definition and flexibility.
Benefits of Chest Fly
  1. Builds Chest Muscles: Strengthens and shapes the pectoral muscles, improving upper body aesthetics.
  2. Improves Flexibility: Enhances the range of motion in the chest and shoulders.
  3. Strengthens Supporting Muscles: Activates stabilizer muscles in the shoulders and arms.
  4. Supports Posture: Promotes better posture by balancing chest strength.
  5. Boosts Upper Body Performance: Improves functional strength for pushing and lifting movements.
